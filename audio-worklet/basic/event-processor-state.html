Event and AudioWorkletProcessorState

A simple demonstration of AudioWorkletProcessorState and how to catch processorstatechange event.

NOTE: Open up the console to see the events being fired.

  • - An instance of TimerProcessor will be in "pending" state after the construction of AudioWorkletNode.
  • - Once TimerProcessor is ready, the state will transition to "running".
  • - The internal timer in TimerProcessor advances when the node is connected to the destination.
  • - When the timer is finished, TimerProcessor reaches to "stopped" state.
